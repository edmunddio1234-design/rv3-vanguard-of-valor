The Structure

Who does what

Shared mission: to serve as a force multiplier connecting rural veterans and their families to earned benefits, transitional housing, and sustainable economic opportunity.

Eagle Enterprises Consortium, L3C (EEC) — the coordinator

The umbrella entity. Coordinates the network, aligns mission with sustainable social enterprise, and maintains accountability across the ecosystem. Houses NetWIRCing Solutions, LLC and the M-TOC (Mobile Triage Outreach Cavalry), and delivers subscription services plus bootcamp technical assistance and training. Visit EEC →

Lighthouse Community Development Corporation (LCDC) — the deliverer

The 501(c)(3)-aligned community development corporation: transitional housing pathways, benefits navigation, and coordinated direct services. When RV3 funds housing or navigation, LCDC is usually the operator on the ground. Visit Lighthouse Rural Communities →

RV3 — Rural Veterans Vanguard of Valor Foundation — the funder (you are here)

The philanthropic arm. Honors service and channels donor, sponsor, and grant support to rural veteran programs — with restricted-fund discipline going in and VI-PAR reporting coming out. About RV3 →

Guardian Angels Enterprises, L3C — the voice and the volunteers

The advocacy, volunteers & communications hub — website, app, podcast, bot, newsletter, blog, and social media — mobilizing volunteers in support of veterans under the banner "Service, honor, legacy," and stewarding the Guardian Light Veterans Legacy Memorial. Visit Guardian Angels →

Follow the Money — On Purpose

How a donated dollar travels

Say you give $2,500 designated to mobile outreach. Here is its journey, hand-off by hand-off.

Step 1 — RV3 receives and restricts

Your gift lands at RV3, is acknowledged in writing, and is booked as a restricted fund for mobile outreach. Accountability at this step: restricted-fund accounting, written acknowledgment, board-reviewed allocation policy.

Step 2 — Allocation against verified need

RV3 allocates the funds to an M-TOC deployment day, scheduled by EEC against the ecosystem's parish-level needs picture. Accountability: documented allocation matched to a defined program plan and deployment calendar.

Step 3 — EEC deploys the unit

The M-TOC rolls into a rural parish with navigators, screeners, and counselors. Every veteran encounter is logged; every referral is routed with consent and documentation. Accountability: compliance safeguards — consent-to-share workflows, accredited-representation boundaries, SOPs.

Step 4 — LCDC catches the referrals

Veterans needing housing pathways or benefits navigation are handed to LCDC's case teams, where follow-up happens within defined timelines. Accountability: referral-completion tracking — routed within 10 business days, tracked to the receiving provider.

Step 5 — Guardian Angels tells the story, VI-PAR proves it

Outcomes post to the VI-PAR dashboard; Guardian Angels reports back to the community through the newsletter and podcast; RV3 reports back to you. Accountability: public dashboard, annual impact report, demo data labeled as demo data.
